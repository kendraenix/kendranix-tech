Phase 3 of 3
Connect Your Domain
6

Add your domain in Netlify

Tell Netlify which domain to use

⌄
  • 1
    In your Netlify dashboard, click your site name to open it
  • 2
    Click "Domain settings" in the left sidebar (or "Set up a custom domain")
  • 3
    Click "Add a domain" and type in the domain you bought — for example yourname.com
  • 4
    Click "Verify" — Netlify will confirm it found your domain and give you DNS records to copy
  • 5
    Netlify will show you two values — a Type A record and a CNAME record. Copy these — you'll need them in the next step.
💡 What are DNS records? Think of them as a forwarding address. You're telling the internet: "When someone types my domain, send them to Netlify." It takes 15–30 minutes to kick in after you set it up.
7

Add DNS records in Hosting.com

Point your domain to Netlify

⌄
  • 1
    Log back into Hosting.com and go to your Domain List
  • 2
    Click "Manage" next to the domain you just bought
  • 3
    Find the "DNS / Nameservers" or "Advanced DNS" section
  • 4
    Add a new Type A record — set Host to @, and paste the IP address Netlify gave you as the Value
  • 5
    Add a new CNAME record — set Host to www, and paste the Netlify subdomain as the Value (it ends in .netlify.app)
  • 6
    Click Save on both records
hosting.com → Domain Manager → DNS Settings

⚠️ DNS takes time: After saving, it can take anywhere from 15 minutes to 24 hours for your domain to fully point to Netlify. This is normal — the internet just needs time to update. Grab a coffee. ☕
8

Enable HTTPS (free SSL)

Makes your site secure — takes 10 seconds

⌄
  • 1
    Back in Netlify, go to your site's Domain settings
  • 2
    Scroll down to the "HTTPS" section
  • 3
    Click "Verify DNS configuration" — once your DNS has propagated, this will turn green
  • 4
    Click "Provision certificate" — Netlify installs a free SSL certificate automatically. Your site will now show https:// 🔒
