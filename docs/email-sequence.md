**Here's what happened to a client before she found me.**

She'd been building her business for 3 years. 4,000 email subscribers. A solid website. A brand she was proud of.

One Monday morning she tried to log into her website — password didn't work.

She tried her domain registrar — locked out.

Someone had gotten into her email account, used it to reset her domain registrar password, transferred her domain to a different registrar, and locked her out completely.

She lost her domain for 6 weeks. Her website was down. Her email sequences stopped working. Her clients couldn't find her.

It cost her an estimated $8,000 in lost revenue and she spent 40+ hours trying to get everything back.

---

**The 3 vulnerabilities that cause this (and the 5-minute fix for each):**

**1. Weak password on your domain registrar**
Fix: Go to Hosting.com right now → Account Settings → Change your password to something 20+ characters with symbols. Enable two-factor authentication. Takes 3 minutes.

**2. No domain lock enabled**
Fix: In your Hosting.com domain manager → find "Domain Lock" or "Transfer Lock" → make sure it's ON. This prevents anyone from transferring your domain without your explicit approval.

**3. Your registrar email is unprotected**
Fix: The email connected to your domain registrar is the master key. If someone gets into that email, they can get into everything. Enable 2FA on that email account today. Use an app like Google Authenticator, not SMS.

Do all three of these right now. Seriously. Close this email, go do it, come back.
